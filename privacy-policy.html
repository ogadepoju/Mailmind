Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

DraftMate is a Chrome extension that generates AI-powered email reply drafts inside Gmail and Outlook Web. This Privacy Policy explains what data we access, how it is used, and your rights as a user. We believe in keeping things simple and honest.

🔒

No data stored

Email content is processed in real-time and never saved to any database.

🚫

Never sold

Your data is never sold or transferred to third parties for any purpose.

🎯

Single purpose

Data is used only to generate your reply draft — nothing else.

✦

You're in control

You review every draft before anything is sent. DraftMate never sends automatically.

1. Who We Are

DraftMate is developed and maintained by Odunayo Gabriel Adepoju, a student at the University of Cincinnati. This is an independent project and not affiliated with Google, Microsoft, or Anthropic. For any questions, contact: ogadepoju@gmail.com

2. What Data We Access

When you open an email in Gmail or Outlook Web, DraftMate reads the following information to generate a reply draft:

Data Why it's needed Stored?
Email subject line To understand the topic of the email No
Sender name / email To address the reply appropriately No
Email body (up to 2,000 characters) To generate a contextually relevant reply No
Gmail OAuth token To send the reply via Gmail API on your behalf No — managed by Chrome only

✓   No email content is ever written to a database or log file. All data is processed in memory during the request and discarded immediately after the draft is returned.

3. How Your Data Is Used

Your email data follows this path when you use DraftMate:

  • Your browser extracts the subject, sender, and body from the open email
  • This is sent via HTTPS to the DraftMate backend server (hosted on Render)
  • The backend forwards it to Anthropic's Claude API to generate a reply draft
  • The draft is returned to your browser and displayed in the side panel
  • No data is retained at any stage of this process

Your data is never used for advertising, analytics, model training, profiling, or any purpose other than generating your reply draft.

4. Third-Party Services

DraftMate uses the following third-party services:

  • Anthropic Claude API — receives email content to generate drafts. Anthropic's use of data is governed by their Privacy Policy.
  • Render — hosts the DraftMate backend server. No email data is persisted on Render infrastructure.
  • Google Gmail API — used to send emails on your behalf when you click Send in Gmail. Governed by Google's Privacy Policy.

5. Google OAuth and Gmail Permissions

DraftMate requests the following Gmail OAuth scopes:

  • gmail.readonly — to read the content of emails you open
  • gmail.send — to send your reply when you click Send
  • gmail.modify — to thread your reply correctly within the conversation

Your Google credentials are never seen or stored by DraftMate. Authentication is handled entirely by Google's identity platform via Chrome's built-in identity API. DraftMate's use of Google user data complies with the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

6. Data Retention

DraftMate does not retain any user data. Email content sent to the backend is held in memory only for the duration of the API request (typically under 3 seconds) and is not written to any storage system, log, or cache.

7. Children's Privacy

DraftMate is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has used DraftMate, please contact us at ogadepoju@gmail.com.

8. Security

All data transmitted between your browser and the DraftMate backend is encrypted via HTTPS. The backend is hosted on Render with industry-standard security practices. While we take reasonable steps to protect your data, no transmission over the internet is 100% secure.

9. Changes to This Policy
